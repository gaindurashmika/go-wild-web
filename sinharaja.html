Sinharaja Forest Reserve

Explore Sri Lanka's pristine rainforest - A UNESCO World Heritage Site teeming with endemic species

About Sinharaja Forest Reserve

Sinharaja Forest Reserve, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and Biosphere Reserve, is the last viable area of primary tropical rainforest in Sri Lanka. Spanning approximately 11,000 hectares, this biodiversity hotspot is situated in the southwest lowland wet zone of Sri Lanka across Sabaragamuwa and Southern provinces.

The name "Sinharaja" means Lion Kingdom, reflecting ancient Sri Lankan legends. This dense forest canopy reaches up to 45 meters in places and is characterized by an extraordinarily high degree of endemism, making it a paradise for biologists, researchers, and nature enthusiasts alike.

Flora and Fauna

Sinharaja is a living laboratory of evolution with remarkable biodiversity. The reserve harbors over 60% of Sri Lanka's endemic trees and woody plants, and nearly 50% of the country's endemic wildlife. Some of the notable species found here include:

  • Purple-faced Langur
  • Sri Lankan Blue Magpie
  • Red-faced Malkoha
  • Green Pit Viper
  • Sri Lankan Junglefowl
  • Numerous butterfly species
  • Endemic tree species like Shorea gardneri and Mesua ferrea

Trekking Experience

The best way to experience Sinharaja is through guided treks on its well-marked trails. Knowledgeable local guides can help you spot camouflaged wildlife and identify rare plant species that might otherwise go unnoticed. The forest is particularly magical in the early morning when many bird species are active and mist hangs between the trees.

Reserve Details

Location: Southwest Sri Lanka, between Southern and Sabaragamuwa Provinces
Best Time to Visit: January to April, August to September
Nearest Towns: Deniyaya, Rakwana, and Kalawana
Reserve Area: 11,187 hectares

Quick Tips

What to Bring: Leech socks, raincoat, binoculars, camera, water
Recommended Clothing: Light, quick-dry clothing, hiking shoes
Important Note: Guide is mandatory for forest entry
