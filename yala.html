Yala National Park

Discover the wild heart of Sri Lanka - Where leopards roam and nature speaks its ancient language

About Yala National Park

Yala National Park, located in the southeastern region of Sri Lanka, is a biodiversity hotspot and one of the most famous wildlife sanctuaries in the world. Spanning nearly 1,000 square kilometers, the park is a mosaic of diverse ecosystems including tropical forests, grasslands, and coastal areas.

Known for having the highest density of leopards in the world, Yala offers an unparalleled wildlife viewing experience. The park is divided into five blocks, with Block 1 being the most popular and accessible to tourists.

Wildlife and Biodiversity

Yala is home to an incredible array of wildlife, making it a paradise for nature enthusiasts and wildlife photographers. Some of the notable species you can encounter include:

  • Sri Lankan Leopard (Panthera pardus kotiya)
  • Asian Elephant
  • Sloth Bear
  • Crocodiles
  • Various species of deer
  • Numerous bird species including peacocks, painted storks, and many migratory birds

Safari Experience

Visitors can explore the park through guided jeep safaris, which offer the best opportunities to observe wildlife in their natural habitat. Early morning and late afternoon safaris provide the most rewarding wildlife viewing experiences.

Park Details

Location: Southeastern Sri Lanka, near Tissamaharama
Best Time to Visit: February to July
Nearest Town: Kataragama
Park Area: 979 square kilometers

Quick Tips

What to Bring: Camera, binoculars, hat, sunscreen, water
Recommended Clothing: Light, neutral-colored clothing
Photography: Recommended, but follow safari guidelines
