Wilpattu National Park

Discover Sri Lanka's largest and oldest national park - Home to elusive leopards and magnificent natural lakes

About Wilpattu National Park

Wilpattu National Park, Sri Lanka's largest and oldest national park, is renowned for its unique complex of over 50 natural lakes called "Villus." Located on the northwest coast spanning the North Central and North Western provinces, this vast wilderness covers approximately 1,317 square kilometers (508 square miles) of diverse habitats.

The name "Wilpattu" literally means "Land of Lakes," referring to the numerous rainwater-filled natural depressions that dot the landscape. These villus are surrounded by grassy plains and dense scrub jungle, creating a perfect habitat for various wildlife species, including the elusive Sri Lankan leopard.

Wildlife and Biodiversity

Wilpattu boasts an impressive array of wildlife in its diverse ecosystem. The park is one of the best places in Sri Lanka to spot leopards, though they require patience to observe. Key wildlife species found in Wilpattu include:

  • Sri Lankan Leopard
  • Sloth Bear
  • Asian Elephant
  • Spotted Deer
  • Sambur
  • Water Buffalo
  • Mugger Crocodile
  • Various bird species including painted storks, eagles, and owls

Safari Experience

Exploring Wilpattu requires a guided safari by jeep, providing visitors an opportunity to witness wildlife in their natural habitat. The park offers a more peaceful and less crowded safari experience compared to some other national parks in Sri Lanka. Early morning and late afternoon safaris provide the best opportunities for wildlife sightings, especially around the villus where animals gather to drink.

Park Details

Location: Northwest Sri Lanka, North Central and North Western provinces
Best Time to Visit: February to October (dry season)
Nearest Town: Puttalam
Park Area: 1,317 square kilometers

Quick Tips

What to Bring: Camera, binoculars, hat, sunscreen, water, light snacks
Recommended Clothing: Light, neutral-colored clothing, hiking shoes
Important Note: Early reservations recommended during peak season
